🚀 Getting Started

Welcome to Gearforged World — a modded Minecraft civilization server running on NeoForge 1.21.1. This isn't a survival or creative server. It's a geopolitical sandbox where the goal is to build a nation, grow it through industry and diplomacy, and defend it (or expand it) through structured warfare.

01
Read the Rules
All of them. Ignorance is not a defence.
02
Join Discord
Required for diplomacy and server announcements.
03
Pick a Civilisation
Or gather allies to found your own.
04
Build & Claim
Build first, then claim. Not the other way around.
New Player Protection

For your first week on the server and until you have diamond armour, other players cannot kill you — unless you initiate combat first. Use this time wisely.

Required Mods

You need a NeoForge 1.21.1 modpack to connect. Ask in Discord for the current modpack link or CurseForge pack ID. Do not try to connect with vanilla or other mod loaders.

⚙

🏛 Civilisations

A civilisation is a player-run nation with its own territory, government, economy, and military. Every player should belong to one — or be in the process of founding one.

Joining an Existing Civilisation

Check the Civilisations page for current factions and their Discord links. Contact their leadership and follow their admission process. Each civ has the right to accept or reject any applicant for any reason.

Founding Your Own

You need at minimum: a location, a build, and claimed land. Then:

  • Build something substantial before claiming — no pre-claiming.
  • Name your civilisation and establish a government structure.
  • Post a declaration in #civpolitics on Discord.
  • Civilisations cannot resemble hate groups or declare themselves fascist.
  • You may use any internal governance structure you choose.
⚠ Leadership Note

Civilisations have full autonomy over membership and internal rules — but all players must still follow the server-wide rules at all times.

Civilisation Rights

  • Exclude anyone from membership for any reason.
  • Use any leadership structure (monarchy, democracy, autocracy, etc.).
  • Declare war on other civilisations (subject to war rules).
  • Build diplomatic relationships and trade agreements.
  • Claim territory proportional to your builds.
⚙

🗺 Territory & Claims

Territory is power. Claims define where you're protected — and where you're not. Getting this right from the start prevents costly disputes.

The Golden Rule

Build first, claim second. You may only claim chunks you have actually built in, plus a 2-chunk buffer radius around those builds. No reserving land for future plans.

Claim Restrictions

  • Do not claim within 5 chunks of another player's claim.
  • No "snake claiming" — long thin corridors of claimed land to connect builds.
  • No low-effort builds (dirt huts, single blocks) used purely to claim land.
  • Do not claim land with intent to block another player's expansion.
  • No pre-claiming areas for future projects.
🚫 Underground Bases

Underground bases are prohibited. Bunkers are permitted only up to 40 blocks below the surface, must be within your claims, beneath an existing above-ground base, and have a usable entrance.

Chunk Loading

The chunk loading feature only works while at least one civilisation member is online. Plan your infrastructure accordingly — offline farms stop running.

🤝 Diplomacy

All formal diplomacy must happen in the right place. This isn't just convention — it's a rule.

Where to Negotiate

  • In-game agreements — must happen within proximity voice/text chat range.
  • Formal treaties, trade deals, declarations — must be posted in #civpolitics on Discord.
  • Casual conversation — anywhere.
📌 Why This Matters

Off-channel negotiations have no standing. If it didn't happen in #civpolitics, it didn't happen officially. Screenshot everything.

Common Diplomatic Actions

Action Channel Notes
Trade agreement #civpolitics Both parties must confirm
Alliance declaration #civpolitics Signed by both civ leaders
Raid declaration #civpolitics Minimum 24h notice required
Total War vote #civpolitics Both civs must vote yes
Peace treaty #civpolitics Required to end Total War
In-game negotiation Proximity chat (in-game) Must be within voice/text range

🏗 Building Standards

Gearforged World has real building standards. Functionality isn't enough — your builds must look like part of a civilisation, not a test world.

The Core Requirements

  • Complete the surrounding structure before building the farm or contraption inside it.
  • All structures must have walls and a roof (unless outdoors is justified — e.g. tree farms).
  • Builds must have genuine decorative effort: details, texture, purpose.
  • Blank walls and flat roofs alone are not acceptable.
  • All Create contraptions and workshops must be incorporated into proper builds.
⚠ No Loopholes

Attempts to argue "technically this isn't a farm" or "technically my machine needs to be outside" will not be accepted. If you're unsure, ask an admin before building — not after.

Landscape Rules

  • Do not ruin the natural landscape unnecessarily.
  • No lava casts.
  • No massive unfilled holes left in the world.
  • Terraforming is allowed as part of legitimate build projects.

Resource Denial

Don't build on the border of another player's claim specifically to deny them resources. Don't mine out all nearby ore deposits to disadvantage rivals. This is a bannable offence if intentional. Exception: temporary assault structures during PvP Time.

🚁 Physics Entities (Aeronautics)

Physics entities are structures assembled in the world and then made dynamic using the Aeronautics mod — turning them into functional airships, warships, submarines, and ground vehicles. They are one of the most powerful (and expensive) tools in the game.

Size & Quantity Limits

  • Maximum size: 200 × 200 × 200 blocks.
  • Maximum quantity: 3 physics entities per player.
  • Both limits are subject to change by admin decision.

Ownership Rules

  • Do not destroy an unoccupied physics entity inside claimed land.
  • Do not steal another player's vehicle for personal use.
  • During battle, if a physics entity is destroyed, salvaging scraps is permitted.

Boarding

  • You may only board another player's vehicle without consent if it is immobilised.
  • "Immobilised" means it cannot move without repairs — simply being parked does not count.

Abandoned Vehicles

  • If abandoned within claimed land: salvage it.
  • If abandoned in the wilderness with no claimed owner: notify an admin to remove the physics properties.
⚠ Performance Warning

Flying too fast into unloaded chunks can cause momentum loss, vehicle disappearance, crashes, or falling from the sky. Use Chunky for client-side pre-generation. All world-border chunks are pre-generated server-side.

Recommended Settings While Piloting

  • Render Distance: 20
  • Simulation Distance: 20 (minimum 10 for slower travel)
  • Disable shaders while constructing physics entities.
  • Players on integrated graphics should avoid physics entity combat.
🚫 World Border

Physics entities can bypass the world border. Doing so will destroy both the vehicle and likely the pilot. Don't go near it.

⚙

⚙ Industry & Create Mod

The Create mod is the backbone of Gearforged World's economy. Mechanical contraptions, automated assembly lines, and factory complexes power every civilisation's output.

Lag Prevention Rules

  • No lag machines of any kind.
  • No extremely efficient farms that noticeably impact server TPS. Spark profiling can be used to identify offenders.
  • Spread farms across multiple cities rather than concentrating everything in one mega-build.
  • One Power Loader per player maximum.

Integrating Farms into Builds

Every farm, Create contraption, and workshop must be integrated into a proper build. The structure around it must be completed first. A working windmill in the middle of a dirt field is not acceptable — house it in a proper mill building.

What You Can Build

  • Automated resource farms (ore processing, crop farming, smelting)
  • Create mechanical assembly lines for weapons and components
  • Power networks using water wheels, windmills, or steam engines
  • Transport networks (Create trains, cart systems)
  • Cannons, autocannon components, and CBC shells
💡 Pro Tip

Build your factory shell first, then design the contraptions to fit the space. It's much easier to plan this way and avoids building standards violations.

⚔ War & PvP

Combat is structured, not chaotic. There are specific windows for base raids, and specific rules for what you can and can't destroy. Understand the difference before you open fire.

PvP Time

Standard PvP Time is Saturdays and Sundays, 12:00–14:00 GMT. During these windows:

  • Players may enter enemy bases.
  • Base protection is disabled.
  • Raids may occur (if declared 24h in advance).

Outside PvP Time, entering another player's claimed land is prohibited.

Raid Declarations

  • Must be posted in #civpolitics at least 24 hours before the raid.
  • Defenders must have time to organise a defence.
  • Each civilisation may declare only one base raid per PvP Time.
  • If the target has multiple bases, specify which one you're raiding.

Griefing Rules

Action Permitted?
Destroying economy infrastructure (farms, machines) ✓ Yes
Damaging or destroying physics entities in combat ✓ Yes
Levelling entire bases ✗ No
Excessive destruction of aesthetic builds ✗ No
Mining through bases manually ✗ No
Breaking farms by hand ✗ No

Total War

If two or more civilisations vote to enable Total War, all normal PvP restrictions between those nations are lifted:

  • PvP Time becomes 24/7 between participants.
  • No raid declaration is needed.
  • Levelling cities and aesthetic builds is permitted.
  • Neutral civilisations remain protected.
  • Total War can only end via a peace treaty posted in #civpolitics.

PvP Ground Rules (Always)

  • ✗ No combat logging.
  • ✗ No Crystal PvP.
  • ✗ No spawn camping.
  • ✓ Players retreating into their base become protected.
  • Infrastructure outside bases (railways, transport ships) may be attacked at any time.

💣 Weapons & Artillery

Modded weaponry is a core part of conflict on Gearforged. Understand the rules before you build or deploy anything that goes bang.

Approved Destruction Methods

  • Modded explosives — from the server's modpack.
  • Autocannons — automated rotary artillery.
  • TNT used as trigger mechanisms for bombs using CBC shell payloads.

TNT Restrictions

  • Maximum 5 TNT per operation.
  • Maximum 2 TNT Minecarts.
  • Raw TNT placement for mass destruction is not the intended use — build CBC payloads instead.

Nuclear Weapons

  • Nuclear weapons cannot be used on claimed chunks outside of Total War.
  • During Total War, nuclear use against participating nations is permitted.

Weapon Testing

  • Do not test weapons within 8 chunks of another player's claim.
  • Find a remote wilderness area — the world is large.

CBC & Autocannon Maintenance

⚠ Always Use Silk Touch

Breaking CBC cannons or autocannons without Silk Touch destroys significant resources. Never attempt to break Levitite blocks. This cannot be recovered, it's just gone.

📈 Progression Path

A rough roadmap from new player to established civilisation leader.

Week 1
Establish Yourself
Join a civilisation (or scout locations to found one). Gather basic resources. Build your first proper structure. Don't die — new player protection lasts one week.
Week 2–3
Claim Territory
Build substantially, then claim. Establish a recognisable base. Begin automating resource collection with Create. Stake your civilisation's position in #civpolitics.
Month 1
Build Industry
Set up automated farms and Create assembly lines inside proper buildings. Establish a production surplus. Begin resource trading with allied civilisations.
Month 2
Military Capability
Construct your first physics entity — a scout airship or ground vehicle. Build CBC cannons and autocannon emplacements. Participate in your first PvP Time event.
Ongoing
Geopolitical Play
Forge alliances, declare raids, negotiate treaties, and expand territory. Build your civilisation's legacy in the world's political history.

💡 Tips & Tricks

Performance

  • Flying at high altitude improves FPS and reduces server strain.
  • Do not maximise Minecraft until it has fully loaded — causes crashes.
  • Disable shaders when building physics entities; re-enable once finished.
  • Use Chunky to pre-generate chunks in areas you plan to fly through.

Politics

  • Screenshot every agreement in #civpolitics. Disputes without evidence go against you.
  • Even if you dislike a civilisation, keep things civil in public channels.
  • Neutrality is a valid strategy, especially early on.

Building

  • Plan your factory layout before building the shell — it's much easier.
  • Build in a style that fits the server aesthetic: industrial, gritty, functional-but-beautiful.
  • If in doubt about building standards compliance, ask an admin first.

Combat

  • Always check your Simulation Distance before engaging in airship combat.
  • Coordinate with teammates during raids — solo raiding a prepared base usually fails.
  • Retreating into your base saves your life. Use it.
📖 Still Confused?

Ask on Discord or check the full rules page. Staff are here to help — but only if you ask before doing something questionable, not after.
